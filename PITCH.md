# CueBoard — Hackathon Pitch

## The 30-Second Hook
"Every day, 300 million people join Zoom meetings. They fumble through menus to mute, dig through settings to share their screen, and lose track of action items the moment the meeting ends. CueBoard turns the Logitech MX Creative Console into the first physical control surface purpose-built for virtual meetings — giving you instant controls, host power tools, and real-time meeting intelligence at your fingertips."

## The Problem (What judges should feel)
- Meeting controls are buried in software menus
- Hosts have no dedicated tools — they use the same UI as attendees
- Meeting notes and follow-ups fall through the cracks
- Existing solutions are all software — nobody has made this physical

## The Solution (What CueBoard does)
Three pages on one device:

1. **Live Controls** — Mute, camera, record, share, react. The basics, made physical.
2. **Operator Mode** — Mute all, spotlight, timer, admit participants. Host superpowers.
3. **Meeting Intelligence** — Flag important moments in real-time. Export a curated summary instantly.

## Why It Wins

### For Logitech
- Showcases the MX Creative Console for a massive market (virtual meetings)
- Uses BOTH buttons AND dial meaningfully on every page
- Demonstrates the device as a professional productivity tool, not just creative software

### For Users
- Zero learning curve on Page 1 — anyone can press "Mute"
- Page 2 solves real pain points hosts deal with daily
- Page 3 is a new category — physical meeting intelligence that complements AI tools

### vs. Competitors
- **Fathom/Fireflies** — AI captures everything and summarizes. CueBoard lets the HUMAN decide what matters. They're complementary, not competing.
- **Stream Deck** — Generic programmable buttons. CueBoard is purpose-built for meetings with context-aware behavior, state feedback, and structured export.
- **Nothing else** — Nobody has built a physical meeting control surface. This is a new category.

## The Story
Tyler is a [role] who runs meetings constantly. He got tired of fumbling through Zoom menus while trying to stay engaged. CueBoard was born from that frustration — the idea that controlling a meeting should be as simple as pressing a button on your desk.

## Key Talking Points for Q&A
- **"Why not just use keyboard shortcuts?"** — You still have to remember them, and they don't give you feedback. CueBoard shows you the state (muted/live, recording on/off, flag count) right on the buttons.
- **"Why not use Stream Deck?"** — Stream Deck is a blank canvas. CueBoard is a purpose-built tool. It's the difference between a universal remote and a custom mixing board.
- **"How is Page 3 different from AI meeting tools?"** — AI tools are the security camera. CueBoard is the detective's notepad. The human is the filter.
- **"What about Teams/Google Meet?"** — Architecture supports multiple platforms. Zoom is fully implemented. Extending to Teams/Meet is just adding shortcut mappings.
