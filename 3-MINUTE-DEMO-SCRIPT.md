# CueBoard — 3-Minute Hackathon Demo Script

> **Time limit:** 3 minutes total
> **Goal:** Show the "wow" features + prove it works + explain why it matters
> **Strategy:** Show don't tell for Page 1 + Page 3. *Mention* Page 2 without demoing every button.

---

## Timing Breakdown (180 seconds)

| Section | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hook | 15 sec | Problem + Solution |
| Page 1 Demo | 45 sec | Live meeting controls (show 4-5 buttons) |
| Page 3 Demo | 75 sec | Meeting Intelligence (the closer) |
| Page 2 Mention | 20 sec | Host tools (talk, don't demo) |
| Closing | 25 sec | Why it matters + unique value |

---

## The Script

### HOOK (0:00 - 0:15) — 15 seconds

> "Virtual meetings are now permanent. We've optimized everything about our workspace — keyboards, mice, monitors — except the actual meeting interface. CueBoard is the first physical control surface purpose-built for virtual meetings. It turns the Logitech MX Creative Console into a meeting co-pilot."

**Visual:** Show the hardware. One quick gesture to the console.

**Why this works:** Sets up the problem, positions the solution, uses physical props.

---

### PAGE 1 DEMO (0:15 - 1:00) — 45 seconds

> "Page 1 is Live Controls — everyday meeting actions you do dozens of times per call. Instead of hunting through Zoom's menus or remembering keyboard shortcuts, it's one button, one action."

**Demo flow (30 seconds of action):**

1. **Press Mute button** → Toast shows "MUTED"
   > "Mute. Instant feedback on the button display — I know I'm muted without checking the screen."

2. **Press Camera button** → Toast shows "Camera OFF"
   > "Camera off. No more 'Can you see me?' fumbling."

3. **Rotate the dial** → Toast cycles: "Reaction: Thumbs Up" → "Heart" → "Clap"
   > "The dial cycles through reactions."

4. **Press dial** → Reaction sends
   > "Press to send. The dial isn't an afterthought — every page uses it meaningfully."

5. **Press Screen Share button** → Zoom's share picker opens
   > "Screen share — opens Zoom's picker instantly."

**Close the section:**
> "This page is speed and muscle memory. Physical buttons beat keyboard shortcuts because you can feel your way through a meeting without breaking eye contact."

**What NOT to demo:** Raise Hand, Gallery/Speaker View, End Meeting (too many buttons, not enough time)

---

### PAGE 3 DEMO (1:00 - 2:15) — 75 seconds (THE CLOSER)

> "Page 3 is Meeting Intelligence — and this is what makes CueBoard different from just a macro keyboard."

**The problem setup (10 seconds):**
> "AI tools like Fathom transcribe everything. But AI doesn't know what *you* think matters. CueBoard captures your curated highlights in real-time."

**Demo flow (50 seconds of action):**

1. **Rotate the dial** → Toast shows "Flag: Action Item" → "Decision" → "Follow-Up"
   > "Dial selects the flag type."

2. **Press Flag Moment** → Toast: "Action Item flagged"
   > "Something important happens in the meeting — I flag it. Timestamped, categorized, non-disruptive."

3. **Press Assign button** → Rotate dial → Shows "Sarah", "Mike", "Dev Team"
   > "Assign it to someone."

4. **Press dial to confirm** → Toast: "Assigned to Sarah"
   > "Confirmed."

5. **Press Add Note button** → Input dialog appears
   > "Add a note: 'Follow up on API design.'"
   > Type quickly, press Enter → Toast: "Note added"

6. **Press Flag Moment again** → Toast: "Decision flagged"
   > "Flag another moment — this time a decision."

7. **Press Export Summary button** → Browser opens with HTML
   > "Export Summary. Watch this."

**While the HTML loads (10 seconds):**
> "This generates a formatted HTML document with all my flags, timestamps, assignees, and notes. It auto-detected Zoom's transcript file and integrated relevant snippets."

**Show the HTML (15 seconds):**
> "Stats dashboard. Flags grouped by type. Transcript sidebar. Action items with assignments. This isn't AI summarization — this is *my* director's cut of the meeting."

**Close Page 3:**
> "Fathom gives you the raw footage. CueBoard gives you the highlights that matter."

**What NOT to demo:** Highlight, Clear Last Flag, Preview, Reset Meeting, Interactive Poll (not enough time; Export is the killer feature)

---

### PAGE 2 MENTION (2:15 - 2:35) — 20 seconds (DON'T DEMO, JUST TALK)

> "Page 2 is Host Power Tools — Mute All, Lock Meeting, Timer for workshop facilitators, Fullscreen, Pause Share, Captions for accessibility. Seven host controls that turn you from a participant into a meeting operator."

**Visual:** Quickly swipe to Page 2, gesture to the buttons, swipe back.

**Why NOT demo it:** Timer takes too long to show, Mute All requires other participants, Lock Meeting requires someone trying to join. Just establish that it exists.

---

### CLOSING (2:35 - 3:00) — 25 seconds

> "CueBoard isn't replacing AI meeting tools — it complements them. Fathom transcribes everything. CueBoard captures what you think matters. Physical buttons make flagging instant, silent, and non-disruptive."

**Pause for 2 seconds, then:**

> "Virtual meetings aren't going away. If we've optimized our keyboards and mice, why not optimize the meeting itself? CueBoard turns meetings from a necessary evil into a controlled, professional workflow."

**Final line:**
> "Thank you. Questions?"

**Visual:** Hands on console, ready to answer questions.

## What Makes This Script Work

### 1. **Show, Don't Tell**
- Page 1: Live demo of 4-5 buttons (30 sec)
- Page 3: Full workflow demo (50 sec)
- Page 2: Talk only (20 sec)

You're demoing ~60 seconds of Page 1 + Page 3, which is realistic and feels fast.

### 2. **The "Closer" is in the Middle**
Page 3 (Meeting Intelligence) is your unique feature. It gets 75 seconds — more than anything else. Judges remember the Export HTML moment because it's visual and impressive.

### 3. **Skip the Boring Stuff**
You don't demo:
- Every button on Page 1 (only 5 of 10)
- Page 2 at all (just mention)
- Raise Hand, Gallery View, End Meeting (not impressive)
- Preview, Clear Last, Reset Meeting (not core value)

### 4. **One Clear Message**
"Physical buttons + human curation = better meeting workflow."

## Backup Plan (If Something Breaks)

### If Zoom isn't detected:
> "I'm running in simulation mode right now — you can see the toast notifications and button feedback. In a live meeting, these trigger Zoom shortcuts."

### If Export fails:
> "I've exported this 50+ times in testing. Let me show you a backup file."
(Have a pre-generated HTML open in a browser tab)

### If the dial doesn't respond:
> "The dial cycles reactions here [gesture], timer duration on Page 2, and flag types on Page 3. Let me skip ahead to the export."

### If judges ask "Why not just use keyboard shortcuts?":
> "Three reasons: discovery, muscle memory, and state visibility. Most people don't know Alt+A mutes. And keyboard shortcuts don't show you if you're muted — CueBoard's buttons do."

## Prop Management

**On screen:**
1. Zoom meeting (you as only participant, or you + your phone)
2. Browser tab ready for Export HTML (will auto-open)
3. Backup HTML file open in another tab (just in case)

**On desk:**
- Console positioned so judges/camera can see your hand movements
- Hands visible at all times during demos

**Pre-flight checklist (5 minutes before):**
- [ ] Zoom meeting started
- [ ] CueBoard plugin running
- [ ] Test one button (Mute) to confirm everything works
- [ ] Export backup HTML open in browser
- [ ] Phone nearby if you want to join as 2nd participant
- [ ] Transcript enabled in Zoom (for Export demo)

## Questions Judges Might Ask (Have Answers Ready)

**Q: "How does this work with Teams or Google Meet?"**
A: "Right now it's Zoom-only — I prioritized one polished platform over three broken ones. The architecture supports Teams and Meet, and I'd add them post-hackathon."

**Q: "Does the Export use AI?"**
A: "No AI. It's local-only — no cloud, no API calls. CueBoard timestamps and categorizes flags as you create them, then generates the HTML. It auto-detects Zoom's transcript file if captions are enabled."

**Q: "What's the business model?"**
A: "Freemium: Page 1 is free forever, Pages 2 and 3 unlock at $5/month or $20 one-time. But for this hackathon, I focused on proving value, not monetization."

**Q: "What's next? What would v2.0 look like?"**
A: "Three big priorities: First, Teams and Google Meet support. Second—and this is exciting—audio playback with Whisper integration. Right now the transcript is text-only. In v2.5, you'd click a timestamp and hear the audio from that moment, with Whisper providing state-of-the-art speech-to-text. All local processing, no cloud. Third, AI-powered meeting intelligence using Ollama—summarization, action item detection, speaker insights. The goal is to stay local and privacy-first while getting smarter."

**Q: "Why not use a Stream Deck?"**
A: "The dial. Stream Deck doesn't have continuous input like the MX Creative Console's dial. Reactions, timer duration, flag types — all need dial-based selection. Plus, the console is designed for productivity workflows, not just streaming."

**Q: "Can you demo the Timer or Lock Meeting?"**
A: "Sure — Timer is on Page 2. [Rotate dial] Set duration to 2 minutes. [Press button] Floating overlay appears with countdown. [Press again] Pauses. [Press again] Resumes. [Clear Timer] Overlay closes."

(If they ask for Lock Meeting, explain: "Lock Meeting requires another participant trying to join. In testing, it opens Zoom's security panel where I can lock/unlock.")

## The Winning Moments (What Judges Remember)

1. **The dial cycling reactions** — Physical interaction, clear feedback
2. **Export HTML opening in browser** — Visual "wow", looks professional
3. **Transcript integration** — Shows technical depth
4. **"Fathom gives you raw footage, CueBoard gives you highlights"** — Clear positioning

**If you nail these 4 moments, you're in the top 6.**

## Practice Tips

1. **Rehearse out loud 10 times** — You should be able to do this script without looking at notes
2. **Time yourself** — If you go over 3:00, cut Page 2 down to 10 seconds
3. **Record yourself** — Watch the recording — are your hands visible? Is the screen legible?
4. **Practice the backup plan** — Know what to say if Export fails
5. **Have a friend watch** — Do they understand what CueBoard does after watching once?

---

## The Most Important Thing

**Judges don't care if everything works perfectly. They care if:**
1. You solved a real problem
2. You built something impressive in the time given
3. You can explain it clearly

Export Summary is your "impressive" moment. The physical dial interaction is your "real problem" moment. Practice delivering both confidently, and you've got a real shot at winning.
